## I would also add a few things that many software agencies miss

### 1. "Numbers That Matter"

Don't hide your achievements inside paragraphs.

```
20+
Commercial Projects

3+
Government Systems

5+
Years Experience

100K+
Users Served

99.9%
Platform Availability
```

People trust numbers quickly.

---

### 2. "How We Work"

Instead of only showing projects:

```
Discovery

↓

Planning

↓

Design

↓

Development

↓

Testing

↓

Launch

↓

Long-term Support
```

Government and enterprise clients love knowing the process.

---

### 3. "Behind Every Project"

Instead of only screenshots:

> Every successful system started with a conversation.

Show sketches, whiteboards, flowcharts, user journeys, testing sessions, deployment photos, and collaboration moments. It makes the work feel authentic.

---

### 4. Technical Excellence (for IT managers)

Create a dedicated page highlighting your engineering practices:

* Secure by Design (OWASP)
* CI/CD
* Automated Testing
* Performance Optimization
* Cloudflare Edge Deployment
* API-first Architecture
* Offline-first Mobile Apps
* Long-term Maintainability

Business owners may skip it, but technical reviewers often look for this before recommending a vendor.

---

### 5. End with a memorable promise

Instead of:

> Contact Us

End with something like:

> **Great software starts with understanding people.**
>
> Tell us about your challenge. We'll help you design a solution that lasts.

---

I think your biggest opportunity is to **position yourselves as a boutique software studio rather than a typical software house**. Most Myanmar software company websites focus on listing technologies ("Laravel", "Flutter", "React"). Yours should focus on **business outcomes and client success stories**, with each project presented as a case study. That approach will differentiate you immediately and make the site far more persuasive to the decision-makers you're targeting.
